Recipe: How to Make Paneer Lababdar

Preparation

  1. 1.Pour 0.5 cup water into a pan or pot. then add the following ingredents
  2. 2 cups chopped tomatoes (about 350 grams)
  3. 4 dried kashmiri red chilies (or any low heat chilies)
  4. 14 cashew nuts
  5. 2 inch cinnamon stick
  6. 4 cloves
  7. 4 green cardamoms

Cooking

  • 1. Preparing the Gravy: Boil Tomatoes & Cashews:
    In a pan, add chopped tomatoes, onions, cashews, ginger, garlic, and green chilies. Add some water and cook until the tomatoes are soft.
    Blend the Mixture:
    Let it cool down and blend into a smooth paste.
    Cook the Spices:
    Heat butter or oil in a pan. Add bay leaf, cinnamon stick, cloves, and cardamoms. Saut√© for a few seconds until fragrant.
    Add Tomato Paste:
    Add the tomato-onion-cashew paste to the pan. Cook on medium heat for 5-7 minutes until the oil separates.
    Add Spices:
    Mix in turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
    Finish the Gravy:
    Add milk or cream to the gravy and mix well. Adjust salt and sugar (if using). Simmer for 5-7 minutes. Add kasuri methi by crushing it between your palms and mixing it into the gravy.

  • 2. Preparing the Paneer:
    Season the Paneer:
    In a bowl, mix turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Add the paneer cubes and toss them to coat evenly.
    Fry the Paneer:
    Heat oil in a pan and lightly fry the paneer cubes until golden. Remove and set aside.

  • 3. Assembling Paneer Lababdar:
    Combine Paneer with Gravy:
    Add the fried paneer cubes to the gravy. Cook for 2-3 minutes to let the paneer absorb the flavors.
    Garnish & Serve:
    Garnish with grated paneer, fresh coriander leaves, and a drizzle of fresh cream, if desired. Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
